What are work activities?

Work activities are the main things you do in your business or work that you want to track. These should be defined in your own words, not categories chosen by your MFI.

Examples:

  • Market vendor: "Weekly market sales", "Restocking inventory", "Customer orders"
  • Tailor: "Tailoring orders", "Fabric sourcing", "Equipment maintenance"
  • Farmer: "Planting work", "Weeding", "Market sales", "Input purchasing"

Your autonomy matters: You can define up to 5 activities that make sense for your work. Your MFI sees these to understand your context, not to judge how you spend your time.

How Daily Signals Work

Once you've defined your activities, you can signal how each one is going on a daily or weekly basis.

The Three Simple Options:
  • Better than expected - Things went better than you planned
  • As expected - Things went according to your plan
  • Worse than expected - Things encountered friction or problems

What signals are for:

  • Letting your MFI know when you're encountering constraints they might help with
  • Creating opportunities for coordination (like adjusting payment timing)
  • Giving your MFI context when problems arise

What signals are NOT for:

  • Measuring how hard you're working
  • Judging your productivity or effort
  • Making decisions about your creditworthiness
Your MFI sees your signals but cannot act on them without offering you support first. All MFI responses are offers you can accept, decline, or request modification on.

Tips for Choosing Activities
  • Use your own language: Name activities in words that make sense to you, not formal business terms
  • Focus on what matters: Choose 3-5 activities that really structure your work rhythm
  • Think about coordination: Activities where MFI support could help (market access, timing, resources)
  • You can change them: Pause activities that are seasonal or add new ones as your work evolves
  • Keep it simple: You'll be signaling on these daily/weekly, so don't make them too complex
